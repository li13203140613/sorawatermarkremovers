Sora 2 Guidelines

The Sora 2 model provides a powerful framework for generating AI video content from text prompts. Following best practices ensures consistent, high-quality outputs.

Effective Prompting

  1. Detailed vs. Open Prompts: Detailed prompts give control and consistency, while open prompts allow creative variations. Balance depends on your goals.
  2. Iterative Process: Small changes to camera, lighting, or action can significantly impact results. Treat each generation as a fresh take.

API Parameters

  1. Model: Choose between sora-2 or sora-2-pro.
  2. Size: Specify resolution, e.g., 1280x720.
  3. Seconds: Set clip length (e.g., 4, 8, 12 seconds).

Prompt Structure

  1. Anatomy: Describe shots as storyboard sketches including camera framing, depth of field, action beats, lighting, and color palette.
  2. Multiple Shots: Keep each shot distinct with its own setup and action.
  3. Length: Short prompts give more creative freedom; longer prompts provide more control.

Visual Cues

  1. Style: Set visual tone early (e.g., "1970s film").
  2. Clarity: Use specific descriptions, e.g., "wet asphalt, zebra crosswalk, neon sign reflection".
  3. Camera Direction: Specify framing and motion (e.g., "wide shot, low angle").

Motion and Timing

  1. Simplicity: Each shot should have one clear camera move and one subject action.
  2. Precision: Describe actions in beats or counts (e.g., "Actor takes four steps to the window, pauses, and pulls the curtain in the final second").

Lighting and Color

  1. Quality and Color: Describe light quality and color anchors (e.g., "soft window light with warm lamp fill, cool rim from hallway").
  2. Palette Anchors: Name 3–5 colors to maintain consistency across shots.

Image Input

  1. Reference: Include images as visual references in API calls.
  2. Formats: Supported formats: image/jpeg, image/png, image/webp.

Dialogue and Audio

  1. Dialogue: Include concise and natural dialogue directly in your prompt.
  2. Background Sound: Suggest pacing with subtle sounds for silent shots (e.g., "distant traffic hiss").

Remix Functionality

  1. Controlled Changes: Use remix to make one change at a time and describe the tweak (e.g., "same shot, switch to 85 mm").
  2. Pinning: Pin a result as a reference and only describe the adjustment to maintain consistency.
